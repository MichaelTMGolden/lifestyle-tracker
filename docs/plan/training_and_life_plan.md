# Training & Life Plan

A 12-month framework for stacking work, climbing, strength, music, and life without burning out. Built around your studio Wednesday, partner time on Fri/Sat nights, and a midday energy peak.

---

## Operating Principles

1. **Stack by energy state.** Slow morning → light/warmup work. Midday peak → hardest physical or cognitive work. Evening → creative music work.
2. **Asymmetric loading.** Climb days don't stack heavy pulling. Harsh vocal days don't stack back-to-back. Studio Wednesday is the lightest physical day.
3. **Recovery is a slot, not leftover time.** One true rest day (Sunday morning is active recovery, not rest). One deload week every 4 weeks.
4. **Seasonal focus over constant push.** Each quarter has a primary peak so you're not chasing four PRs simultaneously.
5. **Capture > schedule for creative work.** Lyrics, riffs, and ideas get logged on phone the moment they appear. Scheduled blocks are for refining, not waiting for inspiration.

---

## Yearly Arc — Quarterly Phases

### Q1 (Months 1–3): Foundations
- **Climbing:** Consistent V4. Hangboard intro (1x/week, 6 weeks in). Footwork drills.
- **Strength:** Build base. Get to 8 strict pull-ups + 8 strict dips. No muscle-up work yet.
- **Music — HIGH PRIORITY:** 
  - **Song 1 complete** (lyrics + melody + demo vocal recorded)
  - Book and attend harsh vocal coach session (month 1)
  - Establish daily vocal warmup habit
  - Record clean vocal reference takes weekly
- **Work:** Coding fundamentals refresh + LLM integration patterns. Audit current system for scaling bottlenecks.
- **Habit:** Lock the schedule. Don't add complexity until rhythm is solid.

### Q2 (Months 4–6): Build
- **Climbing:** V5 attempts becoming sends. Hangboard 2x/week.
- **Strength:** Muscle-up progressions begin (high pull-ups, false grip, transitions). 10+ pull-ups.
- **Music:** Songs 2 + 3 completed. Harsh vocals integrated into 1 song.
- **Work:** Deep on LLM integrations — ship production feature. Start GCP architecture study.
- **Deload weeks:** Don't skip. They're the reason Q3 works.

### Q3 (Months 7–9): Peak Push
- **Climbing:** V5 consistent, V6 projecting begins.
- **Strength:** First muscle-up attempts (banded → unassisted).
- **Music:** Songs 4 + 5 completed. Recording quality dialed in.
- **Work:** GCP infrastructure rebuild — scale path to 100k users defined and underway.

### Q4 (Months 10–12): Send & Consolidate
- **Climbing:** V6 send window.
- **Strength:** Clean muscle-up rep.
- **Music:** Song 6 complete. All 6 songs vocally finished.
- **Work:** System validated for 100k. Document architecture. Plan next year.

## Monthly Rhythm

**Weeks 1–3:** Build. Hit the plan.
**Week 4:** Deload — climb at 60% intensity (technique day, no projecting). Lifting at 60% volume. Vocals: clean only, no harsh. Songwriting: review and refine existing material rather than generate new.

**Last Sunday of month — 45 min review:**
- Did I hit my training sessions? Music sessions? Upskilling hours?
- What's the bottleneck right now (sleep, energy, time, technique)?
- One adjustment for next month. Just one.

## Domain Programming

### Climbing (V6 in 12 months)

**Three session types, rotated:**

1. **Technique day** (Mon)
   - 15 min warmup (easy traverses, mobility)
   - 20 min drills (silent feet, straight arms, flagging, hip positioning)
   - 30 min volume — climb 15–20 problems 2–3 grades below max
   - 10 min antagonists (push-ups, external rotation)

2. **Project / limit day** (Fri)
   - 20 min progressive warmup up to within 1 grade of max
   - 45 min on a single project at your limit — 4–6 attempts max, full rest between
   - 15 min easier volume to flush
   - 10 min antagonists

3. **Light/technique day** (Wed)
   - Movement quality only. Climb 1–2 grades under max. No max effort.

**Hangboard:** Introduce week 6 of Q1. Start with 1x/week, 20-mm edge, 7-on/3-off repeaters. Add second session in Q2. Stop if elbows or finger pulleys complain — this is the most common injury vector.

### Strength + Muscle-up

**Tue — Push + Muscle-up prep:**
- Bench or overhead press 4×6
- Dips 4×6–8 (weighted as able)
- High pull-ups (chest to bar) 4×3–5
- Explosive band-assisted pull-ups 3×3 (Q2+)
- Core: ab wheel or hanging leg raises 3×8

**Thu — Pull + Legs:**
- Pull-ups 4×6–8 (weighted as able)
- Row variation 4×8
- Squat or deadlift (alternate weeks) 4×5
- Romanian deadlift 3×8
- Calves + grip

**Sat — Weak points / full body:**
- Address whatever's lagging. Often: posterior chain, shoulder stability, single-arm work.
- Add false-grip hangs (Q2+) and transition work (Q3+).

### Cardio + Mobility

- **Tue morning:** 30 min zone 2 (easy jog, bike, or fast walk — nasal breathing pace).
- **Sun morning:** 45–60 min zone 2 hike or steady cardio.
- **Daily mobility:** 10 min morning. Focus rotation: Mon hips, Tue thoracic, Wed shoulders, Thu ankles/feet, Fri wrists/forearms. Pick a YouTube routine and don't overthink it.

### Vocals

**Daily distributed practice:**
- **Morning (25 min):** Foundational warmup + technique. Lip trills, sirens, straw phonation, octave slides, scales, gentle range work. This is your non-negotiable baseline. Stacked with mobility.
- **Tue / Fri dedicated sessions (45–60 min):** Focused work during midday when you have energy and aren't being heard by anyone. See below.

**Dedicated vocal sessions (Tue + Fri):**

**Tue — Clean technique + melody work (45–60 min):**
- 10 min refresh from morning warmup (siren, octave slides)
- 20 min on current song's melody — singing phrases in various styles, finding the pocket, testing range limits
- 15 min targeted exercises for whatever's needed (range extension, power, precision, vibrato)
- 10 min cool-down + vocal nap

**Fri — Harsh vocal development (45–60 min, pre-studio prep):**
- 15 min clean warmup (critical — never harsh without thorough clean prep, builds on morning foundation)
- 10 min transition into fry (the safest entry to screams/growls)
- 15 min actual harsh work — short holds, different intensity levels, always stopping before strain
- 10 min clean cool-down + vocal nap (30 min after, no talking/whispering)
- **Enforce the no-talking rule for 30 min post-harsh.** This is where voices get injured.

**Studio Wed notes:**
- You arrive with a warmed voice (morning baseline + songwriting afternoon)
- No harsh work Wed, so you're fresh for the session
- Record any reference vocals or demos you need

**Vocal coach (month 1):** Book now. Especially before ramping harsh work. One session with Melissa Cross or a local coach prevents months of recovery from injury.

### Guitar (Metal rhythm + solos/improv)

**Daily foundation:**
- **Morning (25 min):** Scales, alternate picking drills, warmup picking patterns. Build dexterity and muscle memory daily. Stacked with mobility + vocals.

**Two focused sessions per week, on top of the daily foundation:**

**Tue evening — Metal Rhythm Focus (60 min):**
- 5 min warmup (open strings, basic picking patterns)
- 20 min alternate picking drills with metronome — focus on mechanical precision and consistency at increasing BPMs
- 20 min palm-mute power chord work — tightness, muting clarity, rhythmic pocket with drum machines/backing tracks
- 10 min riff transcription or replication from metal bands you admire
- 5 min cool-down

**Sun afternoon — Lead/Solo/Improv (45–60 min):**
- 5 min warmup (scales, finger exercises — builds on morning work)
- 15 min scale work in context — minor pentatonic + full minor scales on a backing track or over your band's existing songs
- 15 min target-note improvisation — pick one note or interval as your "resolve" and improvise around it to land on that note
- 12 min transcribe one metal solo (a few bars, not the full solo initially) — mirror the phrasing and feel
- 5–10 min free improvisation over a backing track (just play, no goal)

**Input:** Monthly, pick one guitarist you want to absorb. Transcribe a chorus and a lead section. Understand their phrasing, vibrato, bend style, etc.

**Pair with band songs:** Improvise over your band's existing song structures using these techniques.

### Songwriting (6 songs in 12 months)

**The reality:** You're writing from scratch (lyrics + melody). That's real work, not refinement. Budget accordingly.

**Four dedicated blocks per week:**
- **Mon evening (90–120 min):** Song composition — melody + chord structure exploration (using keyboard, sung ideas, or existing progressions). Initial melody sketching.
- **Wed afternoon (60–90 min):** Lyric writing on the current song focus. Work without instruments — force yourself to sit with the melody, not hide in instrumental work.
- **Thu evening (60–90 min):** Integration — marry the lyrics to melody. Record a rough vocal demo. Refine.
- **Sat afternoon (120–150 min):** Production work — clean up structure, harmony ideas, decide on song arrangement for band, or start a new song if one is "done."

**Capture system (unchanged):** Phone notes for lyric fragments, voice memos for melodies. Tag with `#lyric`, `#melody`, `#concept`. Review every Sun for new ideas to integrate.

**Timeline to 6 songs (realistic):**
- **Months 1–2:** Song 1 (lyrics + melody + demo recording). You're building the process, not fast yet.
- **Month 3:** Song 2.
- **Months 4–5:** Song 3 + Song 1 refinement based on band feedback from studio sessions.
- **Months 6–7:** Song 4.
- **Months 8–9:** Song 5 + Song 2–3 refinement.
- **Months 10–12:** Song 6 + all vocal recordings.

**Wed studio sessions are feedback loops:** Bring rough demos. Band input shapes iteration. You don't carry revisions alone.

**Input diet matters:** You're reading daily. For songwriting specifically, bias toward:
- Lyrics from artists in your genre (save 3–4 songs you love, dissect them monthly)
- Poetry (at least 15 min/week, even if brief)
- Your own life (journaling before songwriting sessions primes emotional material)

**Guitar practice in this model:**
- Riff discovery happens in songwriting blocks (Mon)
- Solo ideas get sketched Mon but developed *for specific songs*, not in isolation
- Sun light guitar (30 min) is optional: only if you're excited about an idea or need a mental break

### Work (Coding + Upskilling)

**Daily split:**
- 4 hours deep work morning — product, hardest problem first
- 1 hour upskilling (the 14:30–15:30 slot)
- 1.5 hours afternoon — comms, lighter dev, code review

**Upskilling roadmap:**
- **Months 1–2:** Coding fundamentals refresh (data structures, system design basics). Pick one book + work problems.
- **Months 3–5:** LLM integration deep dive — prompt engineering, RAG, evals, structured outputs, cost/latency optimization.
- **Months 6–8:** GCP architecture — Cloud Run, Pub/Sub, Firestore/Spanner tradeoffs, IAM, observability. Get the Associate Cloud Engineer cert if it maps to your stack.
- **Months 9–12:** Scaling for 100k users — load testing, caching strategy, DB optimization, async patterns. Apply directly to the product.

**Sole-dev hygiene:** Document as you build. Write a one-page architecture overview now and update monthly. Future you (and future hires) will need it.

### Nutrition + Meal Prep

**Sunday prep (90–120 min):**
- 2 proteins: e.g., 1kg chicken thighs + 600g salmon or ground beef
- 2 carbs: rice cooker batch + roasted potatoes/sweet potato
- 2 veg: roasted tray (broccoli, peppers) + raw prep (cucumber, carrots)
- Sauce/flavor: chimichurri, tahini-lemon, peanut-soy — rotates the same proteins
- Portion into 4–5 containers for lunches

**Wed top-up (30 min):** Cook one fresh thing — eggs + greens, stir-fry, etc. Avoids 5 days of identical meals.

**Macro targets (rough):**
- Protein: 1.6–2g per kg bodyweight (muscle gain + recovery)
- Eat the heaviest meal in the 2 hours after training
- Pre-climb: light, 1.5–2 hours before, mostly carb
- Hydration: ~3L/day, more on training days

**Vocal-aware eating:**
- No dairy the day of harsh vocals (mucus)
- No big spicy/acidic meals within 3 hrs of singing (reflux kills voices silently)
- Hot water + honey + ginger before harsh sessions

### Reading

You already read daily — protect that. Rotate the stack:
- 1 fiction (lyric/voice input)
- 1 technical (current upskilling area)
- 1 long-form / poetry / interviews (general input)
- Audiobooks on Sun cardio and meal prep

## Review Cadence

**Daily (2 min, before bed):** Did I do the deep work block, the training, the music block? Yes/no per item.

**Weekly (Sun, 20 min):**
- Wins this week
- Misses + why
- One adjustment for next week
- Plan Mon–Sat by block

**Monthly (last Sun, 45 min):**
- Are the Q-phase goals on track?
- Any injury signals to address?
- One thing to add, one thing to cut

**Quarterly (~90 min):**
- Reset goals for next phase
- Honest assessment: what's working, what's theater
- Adjust the whole template if needed

## What's Likely to Break First

In order of probability:
1. **The music block on tired days.** Pre-decide minimum viable session: even 20 min counts.
2. **Sunday meal prep when the week was hard.** Have a fallback: order from one specific healthy place.
3. **Cardio.** It's the lowest-status item and the first cut. Anchor it to mornings you already have habits.
4. **Mobility.** Stack it with coffee brewing — 10 min on the floor while the kettle boils.
5. **Sleep slipping later than 22:30.** This breaks everything else. Treat 23:00 as the hard ceiling.

More importantly, you've built a sustainable system you can run for years — which is the actual goal.
