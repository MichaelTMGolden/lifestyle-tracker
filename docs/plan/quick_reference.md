## Training Quick Ref

### Climbing Protocol

**Technique Day (Mon):**
- 15 min warmup (easy traverses)
- 20 min drills (silent feet, straight arms, flagging)
- 30 min volume (15–20 problems, 2–3 grades below max)
- 10 min antagonists (push-ups, external rotation)

**Project Day (Fri):**
- 20 min progressive warmup to within 1 grade of max
- 45 min on project — 4–6 max attempts with full rest
- 15 min easier volume
- 10 min antagonists

**Light Day (Wed):**
- Movement quality only. 1–2 grades below max. No max effort.

**Hangboard (Q1 week 6+):**
- 1x/week to start (20mm edge, 7-on/3-off repeaters)
- Add 2nd session in Q2
- Stop if elbows/fingers complain

### Lifting Protocol

**Tuesday (Push + Muscle-up prep):**
- Bench or overhead press 4×6
- Dips 4×6–8 (weighted)
- High pull-ups 4×3–5
- Explosive band pull-ups 3×3 (Q2+)
- Core: ab wheel or leg raises 3×8

**Thursday (Pull + Legs):**
- Pull-ups 4×6–8 (weighted)
- Rows 4×8
- Squat or deadlift (alternate weeks) 4×5
- Romanian deadlift 3×8
- Calves + grip work

**Saturday (Weak points):**
- Address lagging areas (usually posterior chain, shoulders, single-arm)
- Add false-grip hangs (Q2+) and transitions (Q3+)

## Music Quick Ref

### Vocal Warmup (25 min daily, all days)

**Basic protocol:**
1. Lip trills (30 sec)
2. Sirens (1 min) — full range
3. Straw phonation (1 min)
4. Octave slides (1 min)
5. 5-note scales (2 min) — starting middle C, go up/down
6. Song passages (remaining time) — your current focus song or scales

**If you only have 10 minutes:** Sirens (1 min) + straw (1 min) + one scale pass (remaining). Still counts.

### Vocal Sessions (Tue + Fri)

**Tue (Clean + Melody) — 60 min:**
- 10 min siren + octave refresh
- 20 min on current song melody (try different melodic approaches)
- 15 min scales/exercises for what you need
- 10 min cool-down

**Fri (Harsh) — 60 min:**
- 20 min thorough clean warmup (sirens, scales, slides)
- 10 min fry transition (lowest intensity harsh work)
- 15 min actual harsh (short holds, vary intensity, STOP if strain)
- 10 min clean cool-down
- **30 min no talking/whispering after**

### Guitar Technique (25 min daily)

**Rotation (daily, in order or choose):**
1. **Scales** (10 min) — any scale you're weak in (often E natural minor, harmonic minor)
2. **Alternate picking drills** (10 min) — metronome, start 60 BPM, every 5 days go up 5 BPM
3. **Warmup picking** (5 min) — just noodling, get hands ready

**Absolute minimum:** Pick any one of these for 10 min. Still counts.

### Guitar Sessions (Tue + Sun)

**Tue (Rhythm) — 60 min:**
- 5 min warmup picking
- 20 min alternate picking drills + metronome (increasing BPM)
- 20 min palm-mute power chords (tightness + clarity)
- 10 min transcribe/replicate a riff you admire
- 5 min cool-down

**Sun (Lead/Improv) — 45–60 min:**
- 5 min warmup (scales, fingers)
- 15 min scale work in context (backing track, your band's song)
- 15 min target-note improv (pick one note, improvise to land on it)
- 12 min transcribe a solo passage (few bars, not full solo)
- 5–10 min free play (no goal)

### Songwriting Sessions

**Mon (Composition) — 90–120 min:**
- Start with melody/chord sketches (no guitar — use voice or keyboard)
- Demo voice memos of melody ideas
- Decide on structure (verse, chorus, bridge?)

**Wed (Lyrics) — 60–90 min:**
- Pure lyric work. No instruments.
- Sit with the melody you have. Write verses/chorus words.
- Capture mood, story, message.

**Thu (Integration) — 60–90 min:**
- Marry lyrics to melody.
- Record rough vocal demo.
- Refine word placement, phrasing.

**Sat (Production) — 120–150 min:**
- Clean up structure.
- Harmony ideas.
- Arrangement (what does the band do?).
- Or: start new song if one is "done."

## Work Quick Ref

### Deep Work Block (8:15–12:00, weekdays)

**Rules:**
- No meetings
- No Slack, email, messages
- Phone on silent, out of reach
- One thing: the hardest problem first

**Checkpoint:** At 12:00, you should have shipped something, debugged something hard, or made real progress. If you spent the time in meetings/messages, next day start earlier or extend the block.

### Upskilling Slot (1 hour per day, mixed in with work)

**Month 1–2:** Coding fundamentals (data structures, system design)
**Month 3–5:** LLM integrations (prompts, RAG, structured outputs)
**Month 6–8:** GCP architecture (Cloud Run, Pub/Sub, Firestore)
**Month 9–12:** Scaling (load testing, caching, DB optimization)

**Format:** Book + coding exercises. 30 min reading, 30 min hands-on.

**Sunday, 8:05–10:05 (120 min total)**

**What you cook:**
- 2 proteins: chicken, salmon, ground beef, etc. (1kg each)
- 2 carbs: rice, potatoes, sweet potatoes (batch cooked)
- 2 vegetables: roasted tray + raw prep (broccoli, peppers, carrots, cucumber)

**Goal:** Lunch is ready to grab. Breakfast prepped. Dinner ready or quick.

## Recovery Quick Ref

### Deload Week (Week 4 every month)

Scale EVERYTHING to 60% volume:
- **Climbing:** technique day only, no projecting
- **Lifting:** 60% volume (fewer sets/lower weight)
- **Cardio:** easy walks instead of Zone 2
- **Vocals:** clean only, no harsh
- **Songwriting:** review + refine, no new songs
- **Work:** normal

**Why:** Recovery feeds the next 3 weeks. Don't skip it.

### Sleep Protocol

- **Bed by 22:30 every night** (except Fri/Sat flexible if out)
- **No screens after 21:00**
- **Darkened room**
- **Temperature: cool** (65–68°F ideal)

### Nutrition for Training

- **Post-climb/lift:** eat within 2 hours (protein + carbs)
- **Pre-climb:** light meal 1.5–2 hours before
- **Hydration:** ~3L/day, more on training days
- **Vocal days:** no dairy day-of harsh vocals (mucus), avoid acidic 3hrs before singing

## Emergency Ref — "What do I do if..."

**I missed this morning's routine**
→ Do it tonight, even if shortened. Tomorrow morning restart normal.

**I'm injured**
→ Scale intensity 50%, don't stop. If pain (not soreness), see doc. Climbing = switch to mobility/core. Lifting = lower weight. Vocals = clean only, no harsh.

**I missed a songwriting block**
→ Add 45 min to another day this week. Sat is most flexible.

**My voice hurts**
→ Stop everything harsh immediately. Clean-only for 2 weeks minimum. Hydrate. Mention in coach session.

**Partner is frustrated**
→ Fri/Sat evenings are non-negotiable. If they're getting cut, the plan has failed. Stop other things, protect this.

**I'm burned out (energy <4/10)**
→ Cut one training day (not music). Scale songwriting to 2 blocks. This is temporary; restart in 1–2 weeks.

**Work is bleeding into training**
→ Protect 8:15–12:00 ruthlessly. Move work earlier or after training. Tell team: "Deep work block, I respond after."

**I didn't hit my 6 hours of work**
→ Debug: Are meetings in the deep block? Are messages stealing focus? Is training running long? Fix one thing tomorrow.
